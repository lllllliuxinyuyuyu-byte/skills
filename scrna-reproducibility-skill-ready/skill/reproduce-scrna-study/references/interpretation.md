# Interpretation and caption rules

## Paper-comparison figures

State:

1. the original paper figure being compared;
2. the included samples and cell total;
3. whether the same reduction and disclosed parameters were used;
4. any unavoidable version or parameter differences;
5. the biological conclusion that is and is not supported.

## New figures

State why the figure was added, what question it addresses, and its interpretation boundary. Do not imply that a new UMAP reproduces an original tSNE.

## Module scores

Describe scores as relative within the analyzed object. Avoid claims that a high score proves functional maturity. Require orthogonal evidence for barrier function, vascular network stability, stress response, or causal ECM effects.

## Statistical unit

When biological replication is insufficient, report descriptive averages, effect sizes, and sensitivity analyses. Do not use cell-level P values as if thousands of cells were thousands of biological replicates.
